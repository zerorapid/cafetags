I have been to both jubilee hills and Banjara hills branches of karafa. Both have high ceiling indoor spaces that are spacious as well as huge outdoor seating as well. Both branches are surrounded by greenery. Both have glass display of some very unique desserts like chocolate babka, eclair, designer desserts, cinnamon bun, cake slices like chocolate raspberry slice, marble cake, tres leches, etc. They offer garlic bread, fries, sandwiches, burgers, pasta and pizza. Beverages include hot and cold coffee, milkshakes, cold brews, etc. In jubilee hills we ordered coffee pecan praline pastry which had several layers and unique flavor. We then ordered chocolate croissant with gold lines and that was good too. We ordered cold coffee and it tasted good. We ordered mushroom toast which was average. In Banjara hills branch we ordered avocado toast on sourdough. It was tasty. We ordered beans dessert which is nice for coffee lovers. Both places prices are high. Definitely worth a one time visit.

The place has huge space with high ceiling with indoor ac seating and outdoor seating. They have a glass display with different types of desserts. This kind of variety is normally not available in other bakeries. The desserts include mango, apricot, coffee pecan, chocolate based desserts. The menu offers open toasts, starters like fries, sandwiches, pizza, pasta, burgers, etc. In beverages they have brewed coffee, hot coffee, cold coffee, frappe, hot tea, etc. We ordered classic cold coffee which had a good consistency and right amount of sweetness. We then ordered mushroom toast which was decent. Then we ordered coffee pecan praline which was unique in taste with coffee, chocolate, nuts, and was good. We also ordered bow croissant which had chocolate filling inside but was a little hard on the outside. The service is good. But only disadvantage was exorbitant prices.
